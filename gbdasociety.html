Brand & communication system

From scattered touchpoints to a coherent identity

Before the redesign, GBDA’s presence across platforms felt fragmented, making it harder for students to understand what the society offered or how to get involved.[file:116]

  • Audited existing channels: visual style, tone, content types, engagement patterns.
  • Defined a simple brand system that works across digital and physical touchpoints.
  • Rolled out templates and guidelines so future content stays consistent.
Brand systems Content strategy Design operations

Execution and results

Implemented the new identity across LinkedIn, Instagram, and on‑campus materials, coordinating with a cross‑functional team to keep everything aligned.[file:116]

  • Social media strategy reached over 100,000 combined views.
  • Secured over $5,000 in funding to support events and opportunities for 500+ peers.
  • Collaborated with university partners and industry professionals to mentor and judge student work.[file:116]
Stakeholder collaboration Metrics‑driven iteration Leadership
Systems & leadership

Designing the way work gets done

Beyond visuals, a lot of the work was designing how projects move: who owns what, how decisions are made, and how feedback flows between teams.[file:116]

  • Set up simple project structures so multiple initiatives could run in parallel.
  • Used regular check‑ins and retrospectives to adapt as the term evolved.
  • Created opportunities for newer students to step into visible roles.

What this means for Futures Lab

Futures Lab is about prototyping AI‑enabled learning experiences in a collaborative, workshop‑like environment.[web:1][web:3] Leading GBDA prepared the ground: coordinating diverse contributions, making work visible, and keeping momentum across many moving parts.[file:116]

The same skills—facilitation, communication, and building systems that others can use—are key when experimenting with new AI tools in a lab setting.[web:1][web:4]
