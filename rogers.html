Case Study · Rogers

Beyond the Seat – Extending the live arena experience

Winning concept from the uXperience Design Jam with Rogers. Worked with a multidisciplinary team to imagine a connected in‑venue experience that uses Rogers technology to make every moment before, during, and after the game feel more personal and memorable.[file:116][web:126]

Role
UX design, brand strategy, storytelling, prototyping
Team
Multidisciplinary student team · 200+ participants in jam
Outcome
Winning concept presented to panel of Rogers judges
Problem & opportunity

How might we extend the game beyond the seat?

Fans spend a lot of time around the game—arriving, meeting friends, finding food, sharing moments— but most of the experience design focuses only on what happens on the ice or field.[file:116]

The challenge was to use Rogers technology to make the surrounding experience feel more connected, more personal, and more worth talking about afterwards—without turning every interaction into an ad or notification.[file:116]

  • Mapped the end‑to‑end journey from ticket purchase to post‑game reflection.
  • Highlighted friction points like confusion in the concourse, missed moments, and social coordination issues.
  • Framed opportunities where lightweight, context‑aware touchpoints could add value.

Constraints that shaped the solution

  • Must integrate in a believable way with existing Rogers infrastructure.
  • Has to work for first‑time fans and season ticket holders, across different comfort levels with tech.
  • Should enhance the atmosphere in‑venue instead of pulling people out of the event.
Journey mapping Service design Systems thinking Industry brief
Solution

A connected layer across the fan journey

The concept introduced a “beyond the seat” layer that quietly supports fans as they navigate the venue, discover relevant moments, and share them with others.[file:116]

  • Contextual prompts for meetups and experiences based on where fans are and who they’re with.
  • Simple, glanceable UI patterns tuned for crowded, high‑stimulus environments.
  • Clear controls so fans can choose how much they want to interact.
Contextual UX Micro‑interactions Information hierarchy

Prototype & storytelling

The experience was brought to life in Figma as a sequence of key moments, paired with a narrative that walked judges through the fan’s perspective first, then the technology behind it.[file:116]

  • Used scenarios to show how different fan types would experience the same touchpoints.
  • Focused the pitch on emotion and value before features.
  • Aligned visual language and motion with Rogers’ brand to keep it credible.

Reflection & Futures Lab fit

What this project says about AI × UX

While this brief wasn’t explicitly about AI, it trained the systems thinking that carries into AI‑enabled experiences: understanding context, constraints, and what “good” looks like before choosing any technology.[web:1][web:3]

For Futures Lab, this mindset is critical when deciding where AI belongs in a learning journey (and where it doesn’t), how to design transparent interventions, and how to pitch experimental ideas to real stakeholders.[web:1][web:4]
