Case Study · Rogers

Beyond the Seat – Extending the live arena experience

Winning concept from the uXperience Design Jam with Rogers. Worked with a multidisciplinary team to imagine a connected in‑venue experience that uses Rogers technology to make every moment before, during, and after the game feel more personal and memorable.

Role

UX design, brand strategy, storytelling, prototyping

Team

Multidisciplinary student team · 200+ participants

Outcome

Winning concept presented to Rogers judges

Problem

How might we extend the game beyond the seat?

Fans spend a lot of time around the game—arriving, meeting friends, finding food—but most experience design focuses only on what happens on the ice or field.

The challenge: use Rogers technology to make the surrounding experience more connected and personal, without turning every interaction into an ad.

  • Mapped the end‑to‑end journey from ticket purchase to post‑game reflection
  • Highlighted friction points like concourse confusion and missed moments
  • Framed opportunities for lightweight, context‑aware touchpoints

Constraints

  • Must integrate believably with existing Rogers infrastructure
  • Has to work for first‑time fans and season ticket holders
  • Should enhance the atmosphere, not pull people out of the event
Journey mapping Service design Systems thinking
Solution

A connected layer across the fan journey

The concept introduced a "beyond the seat" layer that quietly supports fans as they navigate the venue, discover moments, and share them.

  • Contextual prompts based on location and companions
  • Glanceable UI patterns tuned for crowded environments
  • Clear controls so fans choose how much to interact
Contextual UX Micro‑interactions Information hierarchy

Prototype & storytelling

Brought to life in Figma as a sequence of key moments, paired with a narrative that walked judges through the fan's perspective first, then the technology.

  • Used scenarios to show different fan types experiencing the same touchpoints
  • Focused the pitch on emotion and value before features
  • Aligned visual language with Rogers' brand
Figma prototyping Pitch design Story arcs
Reflection

What this says about AI × UX

While this brief wasn't explicitly about AI, it trained the systems thinking that carries into AI‑enabled experiences: understanding context, constraints, and what "good" looks like before choosing any technology.

For Futures Lab, this mindset is critical when deciding where AI belongs in a learning journey, how to design transparent interventions, and how to pitch experimental ideas to real stakeholders.
