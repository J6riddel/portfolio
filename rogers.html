Rogers - Beyond the Seat

uXperience Design Jam Winner | October 2024

Competed against 200+ students at the University of Waterloo Stratford School to redesign Rogers' Beyond the Seat loyalty program.

The Challenge

Rogers' Beyond the Seat loyalty program faced a critical 98% customer awareness gap despite years of rollout. The challenge was to design an outreach and brand strategy to increase engagement and visibility for this rewards program that provides exclusive access to sports and entertainment experiences.

My Role & Contributions

  • Probed questions to Rogers mentors to understand business constraints and opportunities
  • Proposed using the design thinking framework with user personas to structure our rapid design process
  • Brainstormed and developed user personas with team members based on secondary research and mentor interviews
  • Analyzed three external loyalty program case studies to identify best practices
  • Integrated Rogers emerging technologies including 5G, Internet of Things (IoT), and augmented reality (AR) into the solution
  • Created a Figma prototype and brand strategy that successfully pitched to 10 Rogers judges

Our Solution

We developed a personalized, gamified mobile app that centralizes reward access, tier upgrades, and community-driven features for Rogers customers. Key features included:

Tiered Exclusivity System

Reward loyalty and drive continued engagement through progressive benefits

Simplified User Experience

Inspired by the most effective loyalty models in the market

Gamification Elements

Build community among Rogers customers through interactive features

Strategic Outreach Initiatives

Improve visibility in local markets with targeted campaigns

Mid-Fidelity Prototype

UX/UI prototype highlighting key features tailored to our user persona

Impact

We reimagined brand loyalty through emotional connection, personalization, and community engagement—shifting Beyond the Seat from a transactional rewards system to a lasting, Canadian loyalty experience.

Our solution earned first place from Rogers' judges at the uXperience Design Jam
