Case Study · Sun Life

Designing a financial literacy tool that feels like a coach, not a lecture

Winning uXperience Design Jam project for Sun Life. Our team created a concept that helps young Canadians build financial confidence by meeting them where they are, tailoring education to their goals, and keeping the experience approachable.[file:116][web:126]

Role
UX design, content framing, interaction design, pitching
Team
Multidisciplinary student team · 200+ participants in jam
Outcome
Winning concept presented to Sun Life judges
Problem & opportunity

Financial literacy is high stakes and emotional

Many young Canadians know that money decisions matter, but the learning resources they find are often abstract, overwhelming, or misaligned with their actual goals.[file:116]

The challenge: design an experience that supports real decisions—like saving, investing, or paying off debt—without drowning people in jargon or removing their sense of agency.[file:116]

  • Identified key mindsets: anxious, curious, confident but under‑informed.
  • Mapped scenarios around specific goals instead of generic “financial literacy”.
  • Looked for moments where small nudges and clear framing could unlock action.

Design principles

  • Goal‑first: Start from what people care about (e.g., “I want to travel”, “I want to pay off this loan”), not from products.
  • Plain language: Explain concepts with straightforward visuals and copy.
  • Coaching, not commands: Make recommendations transparent and editable.
Behavioural framing Information architecture Trust & transparency
Solution

Goal‑based flows and tailored learning paths

The concept used a simple, branching flow: pick a goal, answer a few concrete questions, then get a path that mixes short lessons, quick checks, and decision points.[file:116]

  • Each step made only one decision at a time visible.
  • Progress indicators focused on “clarity gained”, not just completion.
  • Visual metaphors helped make abstract ideas like risk and time horizon concrete.
Goal‑oriented UX Content chunking Visual explanation

Prototype & pitch

The Figma prototype walked judges through a representative user journey, showing how the tool would adapt as a user’s understanding and confidence grew.[file:116]

  • Storyboarded the before/after of someone avoiding decisions vs. taking informed action.
  • Aligned the look and feel with Sun Life’s emphasis on trust and long‑term relationships.
  • Used the pitch to highlight how this could plug into existing digital ecosystems.
Figma prototyping Narrative design Stakeholder alignment
Reflection & Futures Lab fit

From financial literacy to learning re‑imagined

This project sharpened a key Futures Lab skill: structuring complex, high‑stakes topics into clear, learner‑centered journeys that could later be supported by AI without removing human judgment.[web:1][web:3]

The same patterns—goal‑based flows, transparent reasoning, and gentle coaching—translate directly to AI‑supported learning tools where models help surface insights, but students stay in control of decisions.[web:1][web:4]
